## Cheating
If you can find a way to cheat the DM may allow a few different mechanical awards depending on the game and method
- Advantage on Roll
- Re-roll
- Add or Subtract up to your Prof Mod from any die
- Add an additional die with your roll

---

## Arm Wrestle
- Each contestant rolls a d20 and adds their Strength modifier.
- Highest total wins.

---

## Darts:
Roll 3 Dex Check. The closest grouping wins. You get 1 reroll per 2 Dex Bonus.

## Drinking Contest
Objective: Last one who has not vomited or passed out.

| Drink | DC | Points |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| Ale | 10 | 1 |
| Liquor | 14 | 2 |
| Moonshine | 17 | 3 |
| Dwarven Ale | 20 | 4 |
| Goblin's Hootch | 23 | 5 |

**Rules:**
- Each drink requires a Constitution saving throw against the listed DC.
- Failing a Save adds the point value of the drink to your Drunk Score. Earn twice the points on a fumble.
  - 2 DS: -2 to Saving Throws
  - 5 DS: The Poisoned
  - 8 DS: Vomit (lose)
  - 10 DS: Pass Out

## Bronze Horde (dice game)
Premise: raid up to 18 coastal villages; your outing score is how many you raid.

**Rules:**
- Roll 3d6 as many times as you like to approach 18 without exceeding it.
- The roll closest to 18 (without going over) is your score for that outing.
- Score target: first player to reach exactly 100 points wins.
- Special: rolling three sixes in a single throw grants an additional +10 points (representing captured slaves).

## Darts
- Players take turns rolling 1d20 and 1d6 both at once, three times. 
- The d20 determines what number they hit. 
- The d6 determines where on that number they hit. 
- First player to 301 wins the pot.
  
> For example, a 1 is the outer ring (double), a 3 is the inner ring (triple), while a 6 is the bull's eye (50).

## Threes Away Dice Game
- Players take turns rolling 5d6. 
- All numbers on the die are face value except for 3's which are worth 0. 
- Lowest score wins the pot.
